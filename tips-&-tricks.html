Tips & Tricks

Playing Valorant for the first time can be frustrating when you feel like you don't know what you are doing! Here are some tips & tricks that you should know in order to play better.

Master a few agents

As of today, there are 23 agents in Valorant for you to choose from. If you are always changing who you are playing as, then you will never feel comfortable playing these characters. It is especially important in the beginning to find one or two characters that you like and really learn how their abilities work. This will make you more confident with their moves which will allow you to focus on strategy.

Crosshair placement is everything

An aspect in the game that is important to master early on is crosshair placement. All of the characters are the same height meaning that if you keep your crosshair at head level at all times, then you won't have to aim and can simply shoot when you see them. Mastering crosshair placement will allow you to turn corners and already be aiming for their heads in order to get easy kills.

Communication is key

Commnicating with your teammates is very important in Valorant. Valorant is a team game and communicating with your teammates will allow you to strategize and be aware of what you can do to better work as a team. It's always a good idea to explain what you plan to do during the round so that others can play with you.

Find the right sensitivity for you

A lot of players don't have the right mouse sensitivity when they start playing Valorant. Having a sens that is too high will not allow you to make the micro-adjustments that are required when the enemy peaks corners. Having a sens that is too low will leave you reacting slower and you will have to move your mouse way too much. I would recommend going into the firing range to practice shooting bots in order to find a sens that works for you.

Your team composition does matter

Valorant has 4 different roles that agents fall under: duelist, sentinel, initiator, controller. It is important to make sure that your team comp includes all of these roles in some way. The basic team composition that is good is 1 controller to smoke off the map, 1 sentinel to watch the flank, 1 initiator to scout the bomb-site, and two duelists to take space and get kills. Team compositions can vary, but you will definately want at least 1 controller and initiator.

Be smart with you economy

It is important to pay attention to your economy in the game. If your teammates are saving, then you should save with them. If they are buying, then you should try to buy with them. Something else to consider is if you lose pistol round, then you should save the next round unless you are positive that you will win if your force buy. Having a good economy can easly be the difference between winning or losing a game.
